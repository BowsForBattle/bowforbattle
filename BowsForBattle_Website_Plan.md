# Bows for Battle Website Plan (Restart)

Date: 2026-06-22

## 1. Website Direction

Primary focus:
- Lead with mission, healing, and veteran connection.

Secondary focus:
- Keep sponsorship and donations visible but not dominant.

Tone:
- Respectful, grounded, and community-centered.
- Avoid aggressive fundraising language.

## 2. Core Message

Bows for Battle supports veterans through the discipline, focus, and healing power of archery. The goal is to help veterans reconnect with themselves, fellow veterans, and the outdoors.

The experience is built around:
- Stance
- Breath
- Focus
- Patience
- Precision

## 3. Homepage Structure

1. Hero
- Mission-forward headline.
- One primary action: Read the Mission.
- One secondary action: See Ways to Support.

2. Mission and Vision
- Full mission summary.
- Vision for reducing isolation and avoidable veteran loss.

3. Veteran Experience
- Explain how events, routine, and camaraderie support healing.
- Highlight core principles and personal growth.

4. Impact Snapshot
- Show a few clear metrics.
- Keep wording factual and simple.

5. Ways to Support
- Present support options at a high level.
- Avoid crowded pricing-first presentation.

6. Support Form
- Clean, minimal form.
- Position as helping advance mission outcomes.

## 6. UX and Accessibility

- Mobile-first responsive layout.
- High contrast for readability.
- Clear heading hierarchy.
- Labeled form fields and meaningful alt text.
- Keyboard-focus visible on interactive controls.

## 7. Copy Guardrails

Do:
- Use mission language first.
- Tie support to outcomes for veterans.
- Keep calls-to-action calm and direct.

Avoid:
- Overusing donation prompts.
- Repeating sponsorship labels too often.
- Marketing-heavy or urgent sales-like language.
